## Purpose
A communication plan ensures that the right information reaches the right people at the right time. Use this template to define communication cadence, channels, and responsibilities for your project.

## Communication Principles
- **Transparency**: Share information openly and proactively
- **Timeliness**: Communicate at appropriate intervals for the audience
- **Relevance**: Tailor content to stakeholder needs and interests
- **Consistency**: Use standard formats and channels
- **Two-way**: Encourage feedback and questions

### 2. Communication Cadence

| Communication Type | Frequency | Participants | Owner | Format | Purpose |
|-------------------|-----------|--------------|-------|--------|---------|
| Daily Standup | Daily (15 min) | Dev team, QA, Scrum Master, PM (optional) | Scrum Master | In-person/video | Share progress, blockers, plans |

| Sprint Review/Demo | Bi-weekly (1 hour) | Full team, stakeholders | Scrum Master | Demo | Show completed work, get feedback |
| Sprint Retrospective | Bi-weekly (1 hour) | Dev team, QA, Scrum Master, PM | Scrum Master | Meeting | Reflect and improve processes |
| Weekly Status Report | Weekly | PM, PdM, Scrum Master, Data Analyst | PM | Email/Doc | Share progress, metrics, risks |
| Stakeholder Update | Bi-weekly or monthly | PM, Stakeholder Champion, stakeholders | Stakeholder Champion | Email/Presentation | High-level status and milestones |
| Metric Review | Weekly | Data Analyst, PM, PdM | Data Analyst | Dashboard review | Review KPIs and insights |
| UX Review Session | As needed | UX Lead, PdM, Developers | UX Lead | Design review | Validate designs and usability |
| Risk Review | Weekly | PM, Scrum Master, relevant stakeholders | PM | Meeting/Doc | Review and mitigate risks |
| Release Communication | Per release | PM, PdM, Stakeholder Champion | PM | Email/Announcement | Notify of release and changes |

### 5. Escalation Path

**Level 1 - Team Level**
- Issue: Day-to-day blockers, technical questions
- Handler: Scrum Master, Tech Lead
- Response Time: Same day

**Level 2 - Management Level**
- Issue: Resource conflicts, scope changes, cross-team dependencies
- Handler: PM, PdM
- Response Time: 1-2 business days

**Level 3 - Executive Level**
- Issue: Strategic decisions, budget overruns, major scope changes
- Handler: Stakeholder Champion, Executive Sponsor
- Response Time: 2-3 business days

**Critical/Emergency**
- Issue: Production incidents, security breaches, customer-impacting outages
- Handler: Incident Commander (on-call), PM, Security team
- Response Time: Immediate

### 6. Meeting Norms
- **Start on time**: Respect everyone's schedule
- **Agenda shared in advance**: At least 24 hours before the meeting
- **Action items documented**: Clear owners and due dates
- **End with next steps**: Ensure everyone knows what happens next
- **Meeting notes shared**: Within 24 hours of the meeting

## Customization Checklist
- [ ] Identify all stakeholder groups for your project
- [ ] Determine appropriate communication frequency for each group
- [ ] Assign owners for each communication type
- [ ] Set up communication channels (Slack, email lists, meeting invites)
- [ ] Create templates for recurring communications
- [ ] Establish escalation procedures
- [ ] Review communication plan at project kickoff
- [ ] Update plan when scope or stakeholders change

## Best Practices
- **Over-communicate during uncertainty**: More is better than less during ambiguous phases
- **Use visual status indicators**: Red/yellow/green for quick status assessment
- **Archive important decisions**: Keep a decision log for reference
- **Solicit feedback on communication effectiveness**: Adjust based on what works
- **Automate where possible**: Use tools to auto-generate status from project boards
- **Respect time zones**: Schedule meetings considerately for distributed teams
- **Be concise**: Busy stakeholders appreciate brevity
